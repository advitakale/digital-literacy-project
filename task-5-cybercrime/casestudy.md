# Case Study — Phishing Attack

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers
disguise themselves as trusted individuals or
organizations to trick victims into revealing
sensitive information such as passwords, bank
details, or OTPs.

## How It Typically Happens (Step by Step)

1. The attacker creates a fake email that looks
   exactly like it is from a legitimate source
   such as a bank, university, or popular website.

2. The email contains an urgent message — for
   example: "Your account will be suspended.
   Click here to verify your details immediately."

3. The victim clicks the link which opens a fake
   website that looks identical to the real one.

4. The victim enters their login credentials,
   password, or bank details on the fake website.

5. The attacker captures this information and uses
   it to access the victim's real accounts or steal
   money.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
Phishing attacks commonly target college students,
working professionals, and senior citizens —
basically anyone who regularly uses email, online
banking, or shopping websites. Students are
especially vulnerable because they frequently
sign up on new platforms and may not recognize
suspicious links.

## Consequences
The consequences of a phishing attack can be
severe. Victims may lose money from their bank
accounts, have their personal identity stolen,
or lose access to important accounts. In some
cases, attackers use the stolen information for
further crimes, making recovery extremely
difficult for the victim.
